The Problem

What is fake news?

Fake news refers to misinformation or disinformation spread through online media — fabricated stories that mimic credible reporting to deceive readers. It spreads faster than corrections, influences opinions, and can have real-world consequences ranging from public health crises to political unrest.

🧨
Disinformation

Deliberately false content crafted to mislead a specific audience for political or financial gain.

🌀
Misinformation

Inaccurate content shared without malicious intent — often by people who genuinely believe it.

🎭
Satire & Parody

Humorous content that can be mistaken for real news when shared without original context.

📢
Clickbait

Sensationalist headlines designed to generate engagement, often misrepresenting the actual content.

Under the Hood

How it works

The app runs a classic NLP pipeline — from raw text input to a trained ML model producing a real/fake verdict.

1
Text Input

The user pastes a news article or headline into the form. The raw text is sent to the Flask backend.

2
Text Preprocessing (NLP)

The text is cleaned — punctuation removed, lowercased, and stop words stripped — using standard NLP techniques.

3
TF-IDF Vectorization

Term Frequency–Inverse Document Frequency converts the cleaned text into a numerical feature vector the model can understand.

4
ML Model Prediction

A pre-trained classifier (e.g. Logistic Regression or Passive Aggressive Classifier) analyses the vector and outputs a prediction.

5
Result Displayed

Flask renders the prediction page showing whether the article is likely REAL or FAKE.
